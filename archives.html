Archival Recovery

The Archives

150,000 court records, two judicial archives, and a century of ordinary people's legal reasoning, recovered and preserved.

Inside the Archive
Image 1 High Court of Uganda records stored in a basement room in Kampala, packed floor to ceiling prior to archival recovery. These records were uncatalogued and at risk of deterioration when they were discovered.
Image 2 Members of the archival team who organized and catalogued the High Court and Mengo Court records. The project involved archivists, academics, students, and court staff working collaboratively.
Image 3 Sauda Nabukenya working inside the High Court archive, Kampala. This photograph was taken during the initial stages of assessing the condition and scope of the collection.
Image 4 Unsorted court records at the Mengo Court prior to organizing and cataloguing. These files document cases heard in Buganda Kingdom courts and had been stored without archival order.
Image 5 Sauda Nabukenya organizing court records on shelves at the Uganda National Archives. This stage followed cataloguing and involved long-term preservation and transfer.
Image 6 Sauda Nabukenya sorting and reading court records during the cataloguing project at Mengo Court. The work involved identifying case types, dates, jurisdictions, and languages of record.
Image 7 The Mengo Court archive filled with records of the Buganda Kingdom. These files include civil and criminal proceedings written primarily in Luganda.
Image 8 Interior view of the Mengo Court archive. The collection documents decades of adjudication in Buganda’s native courts.
Image 9 A bundled set of Buganda Kingdom court records. Files were originally stored in tied bundles before archival rehousing.
Image 10 Buganda Kingdom court record from the Ggombolola Enkulu Ey’Olukiko, Mmengo, written in Luganda. These files record the names of complainants, defendants, witnesses, and presiding judges, and document everyday legal reasoning in Buganda’s native courts.
