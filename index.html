I am a legal and social historian of modern Africa. My research focuses on histories of law, gender, class, labor, and property in colonial Africa and the British Empire, with particular attention to how law operated in everyday life. Drawing on more than 150,000 court records that I discovered and helped preserve, my forthcoming book, The People and Their Pursuit of Justice, Right, and Peace, recovers how ordinary Ugandans shaped law through vernacular reasoning and local practices.
